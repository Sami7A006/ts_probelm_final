🏛️ Event Description

BuildFuture is a hands-on civil engineering hackathon where student teams design, analyze, construct, and test bridge models using limited materials provided by the organizers. The challenge encourages participants to apply structural engineering principles, creativity, teamwork, and practical problem-solving skills.

Teams must develop a bridge that spans the specified distance and can sustain the maximum possible load while using materials efficiently. The hackathon simulates a real engineering workflow across six stages.

🌉 Permitted Bridge Types
━Beam Bridge
🔺Truss Bridge
🌀Arch Bridge
🔗Suspension-Inspired Bridge
⚗️Hybrid Bridge Systems

Teams must clearly declare their chosen bridge type before the load testing stage begins.

🏗️ Challenge Stages
1
Concept Development
Analyze the challenge, explore bridge configurations, determine load transfer paths, and finalize conceptual design strategy.
2
Structural Design
Convert concept into a detailed design sheet — bridge type, sketch, structural reasoning, and identification of major structural members.
3
Model Construction
Build the bridge using provided materials, focusing on accurate alignment, strong joints, structural symmetry, and efficient material use.
4
Optimization & Refinement
Review and strengthen critical joints, improve bracing, adjust components, and reduce unnecessary material weight before testing.
5
Load Testing
Bridge placed on supports; load applied gradually at mid-span until failure. Judges record maximum load sustained, failure location, and failure mode.
6
Final Presentation
Present structural concept, bridge type, load transfer mechanism, expected vs actual performance, and lessons learned to the evaluation panel.
🧱 Permitted Materials
  • Popsicle / ice-cream sticks (organizer-provided)
  • Fevicol adhesive (organizer-provided)
  • Thread — if permitted by organizers
  • Sticks may be cut or trimmed to suit the design
🚫 Not Permitted
  • Metal components of any kind
  • Tape or external reinforcements
  • Additional adhesives beyond provided Fevicol
  • Any external or pre-brought materials
📄 Design Sheet — Required Contents
  • Bridge type selected
  • Sketch of the bridge structure
  • Structural reasoning behind the design
  • Identification of major structural members
  • Expected load path and potential failure points

Teams may use hand sketches, diagrams, or structural analysis tools to justify their design decisions. The design sheet must be submitted before the load testing stage.

⚖️ Judging Criteria
💪

⭐ Bonus Points
  • Sustainability — Efficient use of materials, minimizing waste, or reusing trimmed pieces
  • Teamwork — Effective collaboration and coordination observed during the construction phase
📋 Key Rules
  • All bridges must be constructed during the hackathon period — no pre-built models
  • Bridge must remain free-standing and structurally stable
  • Bridge must span the prescribed distance set by organizers
  • Only organizer-provided materials may be used
  • Bridge type must be declared before load testing begins
  • Judges' decisions on load test results are final and binding
